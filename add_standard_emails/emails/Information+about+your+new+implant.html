Dental implants are the ideal way to replace missing teeth and restore your mouth’s natural ability to comfortably chew and eat, while giving your mouth the stability it needs to bite properly. It is also the best way to ensure the long-term health of the rest of your mouth.

 A dental implant is a synthetic tooth root that looks like a bolt, placed in your jawbone. The “root” is usually made of titanium (the same material used in many replacement hips and knees), a metal that is well-suited to pairing with human bone. A replacement tooth or dental crown is then attached on top of the “root.”

Dental implants are preferable to conventional bridges and dentures — they look more natural, feel and act more like natural teeth, and give you a much stronger bite. Best of all, people love the comfort and confidence that dental implants provide. And because they don't rely on neighboring teeth for support, implants don't compromise the health of your natural teeth. In fact, while bridges are only expected to last 7-10 years, implants can last a lifetime. That is a great investment in your health and your quality of life.
