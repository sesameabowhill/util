At your appointment, we discovered periodontal disease, the first stage of gum disease (also known as gingivitis).  Periodontal disease is an infection in the gums, deep tissues, and bones that support your teeth. (“Periodontal” means “around the tooth.”) The bacteria in the built-up plaque on your teeth are producing poisons, or toxins, that irritate the gums, causing infection. It is similar to the irritation and infection around a splinter in your hand. Just as the infection tries to break down the bacteria around the splinter and push it out of your body, periodontal infection tries to fight the bad bacteria in your gums and breaks down your bones and tissues that hold your teeth in place.

The earlier gum disease is treated, the less damage it can potentially do to your teeth and your overall health.  We want to do everything we can to help you get the treatment you need.
