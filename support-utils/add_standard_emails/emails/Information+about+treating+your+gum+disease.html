We enjoyed having you in our office today. As you know, our goal is the same as yours: to help you gain and maintain a healthy smile for a healthy life.

Today we scheduled your next appointment for treatment of gum disease, also known as periodontitis.  Periodontitis is an infection of the gums, tissues, and bones that support your teeth. (“Periodontal” means “around the tooth.”) We observed “pockets” or spaces where your infected gums have begun to separate from your teeth. If left untreated, these pockets can grow larger, causing damage to your gums and eroding the bone around your teeth.   It’s like the foundation of a house that is crumbling and eroding.  Just as a safe and secure home must have a solid foundation, your teeth need healthy gums and bones to hold then in place for life. You made the right treatment decision to strengthen your “foundation” so that you won’t lose your “house” (your teeth).




When you come for your appointment, we will remove the plaque and tartar from your teeth where a regular cleaning can’t reach: under the gum line or around the “foundation” of your teeth. It is similar to the difference between washing your car and having it detailed.  Detailing reaches the most difficult areas, leaving things fresh and clean again. Just like “detailing,” we will be leaving a healthy, clean surface on your teeth above and below the gum line.   That way it will be easier for the gum tissue to reattach to the tooth.
